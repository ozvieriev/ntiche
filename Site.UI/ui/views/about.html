Activity Abstract: Cancer-related fatigue (CRF) is one of the most common and distressing symptoms experienced by individuals affected by cancer. This distressing and persistent sense of tiredness or exhaustion affects patients’ activities of daily living and reduces their quality of life. Fortunately, there are many things that can be done to reduce CRF and improve patients’ general well-being. This modular learning program developed by a panel of experts from across Canada outlines a multidisciplinary management approach for CRF. The overview reviews what CRF is, how to assess CRF, and how to treat CRF. Additional learning activities are available to delve deeper into the various interventions known to help improve CRF, including physical activity, psychological well-being, sleep, spirituality, and nutrition. Supplemental patient materials are also available to help discuss treatment options with your patients and help them create personalized action plans to manage their fatigue and help them feel better.

Target audience: This educational activity is intended for oncologists, oncology nurses, primary care physicians, psychologists, spiritual health practitioners, physiotherapists, kinesiologists, dietitians, pharmacists, and other healthcare professionals involved in the care of patients with cancer.

Learning Objectives

  • Overview
  • Describe the prevalence and impact of cancer-related fatigue (CRF)
  • Discuss appropriate screening and assessment of CRF
  • Recommend or refer to physical activity, psychosocial, sleep, spiritual, and nutrition-based interventions that can help reduce CRF;
  • Recognize the role of a multidisciplinary team approach in managing CRF and tailoring interventions to the needs of the individual patient
  • Physical Activity & Cancer-Related Fatigue
    • Assess how physical activity can improve fatigue in cancer patients
    • Recognize who would benefit most from physical activity/exercise-based interventions
    • Discuss the type, duration, frequency, and intensity of exercise strategies that would benefit cancer patients
  • Sleep & Cancer-Related Fatigue
    • Compare sleepiness and fatigue in the context of cancer
    • Review strategies to modify maladaptive sleep behaviours and beliefs in order to improve sleep outcomes and CRF
    • Identify the role of sleep medication in patients with cancer who have difficulty sleeping
  • Spirituality & Cancer-Related Fatigue
    • Describe what spirituality is and its importance to help cancer patients cope with their diagnosis and CRF
    • Discuss how to assess and address the spiritual needs of patients with cancer
    • Recognize spiritual-based interventions that can help reduce CRF, including mind-body interventions
  • Nutrition & Cancer-Related Fatigue
    • Discuss the relationship between nutrition and fatigue in cancer patients
    • Review emerging studies on how nutrition can help reduce cancer-related fatigue
    • List strategies patients and caregivers can mploy on how to eat when fatigued
